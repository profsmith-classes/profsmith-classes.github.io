Grading Policy and Assignments

syllabus

Grading Policy

Grading Scale

This course follows the standard CSUF grading scale.

Grade Breakdown

Assignment Weight
Values Exercise 20%
Exams (2) 40%
Reflection Essays (2) 40%

WU Grades

If you do not attempt an assignment or do not take an exam, you will receive a WU (Withdrawal Unauthorized) as your class grade. WU is calculated as an F for your GPA, but unlike an F, it indicates that you did not submit work that could be evaluated. It is critical that you attempt each assignment.

See the CSUF Catalog for complete details.

Missed and Late Work Policy

  • Each student will have one make-up exam opportunity for missed exams without documented justification. The make-up exam will be shorter and carry a 5% score reduction.
  • Essays submitted late have a 5% reduction per day, up to a maximum of 30%.
  • This course has no meeting time and no attendance requirement.
  • No extra credit assignments are available.
  • All assignments are completed individually.

Course Assignments and Requirements

All course assignments and exams will be completed on Canvas.

Discover Your Values (20%)

This class explores environmental health from multiple perspectives. Our personal values are a key part of understanding how we see, value, and interact with the environment. This assignment will ask you to spend time thinking about your own core values.

Exams (40%)

There will be two exams, each worth 20% of your total grade. Exams are multiple choice.

Reflection Essays (40%)

Personal Reflections on Environmental Interconnectedness

There will be two reflection essays, each worth 20% of your grade. These assignments aim to deepen your understanding of the intricate relationship between human life and the environment. You are encouraged to reflect critically on how the concepts presented in the class might influence your thinking, lifelong learning, and your personal relationship with the environment.

Grading Criteria: Each essay has detailed instructions and a rubric attached in Canvas. Please review them carefully.

Technical Issues

Canvas Issues

Issues involving Canvas access, submissions, quizzes, or grades must be reported to me immediately. After notifying me, contact campus IT Services (ITS) and follow their procedures. Documentation from ITS may be required. Canvas problems reported after a deadline, without prior notice or documentation, will not be accommodated.

Personal Technical Problems

Problems with your own computer, internet connection, software, file corruption, browser issues, time zone settings, or general device failure are not grounds for extensions or exceptions. You are expected to maintain reliable access to a functioning computer and internet connection for an online course.

Timing and Documentation

Technical issues must be reported as soon as they occur. Waiting until or after a deadline substantially limits what can be done. Screenshots, error messages, timestamps, or ITS documentation may be required.

Bottom Line

This is an asynchronous online course. Managing your technology is part of managing the course. Legitimate system failures will be handled fairly when reported promptly and through the correct channel. Personal technical failures remain your responsibility.

Class AI Policy

You are strictly prohibited from using Generative AI (GenAI) or Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, and Grok, for research or writing assignments. Any submitted work that has obviously used such tools will receive a failing grade. You are also prohibited from submitting (or inputting) any course material to a GenAI or LLM service, especially an online one.

The writing assignments in this course ask you to think about what you feel about a topic. They are designed for you to reflect on your own values. AI cannot perform this task.

There are several reasons for this policy:1

  • The written assignments are designed to teach you how to think critically and reflect on your own values. This includes learning to do your own research, developing your own writing style, and being able to communicate through writing. Research, writing, and communication are thinking. You aren’t thinking and you aren’t learning to think critically if you aren’t doing the research, writing, and communicating. (Perfors 2024; Barshay 2024; Bastani et al. 2024; Chiang 2024; Lee et al. 2025)

  • Do not submit any course material to an LLM. You do not have permission from the authors/creators to do so. LLMs will use submitted material to train their models, effectively taking ownership of that material. (OpenAI, n.d.)

  • LLMs are not search engines, databases, or indexes. They are systems for generating sequences of words statistically probable given a prompt. Use the readings provided in the course, and learn to use the library and its databases. Be aware that using AI as a search engine is bad for information verification, information literacy, and serendipity. (Bender 2024b; Shah & Bender 2022; Shah & Bender 2024)

  • Do not turn in the output of an LLM in this course. Turn in your own work instead. I cannot express how little I care about an LLM’s output to a prompt on the assignments for this course. I am, however, very interested in your writing, your thoughts, and how you answer the assignments.

  • LLMs are not “intelligent.” They have no reasoning capability or understanding. As purely stochastic systems, they are unable to differentiate between facts and falsehoods, reality and unrealities, truth and lies. An LLM could produce passing work for this course — but it could also (and this is more likely) produce nonsensical arguments, made-up references, and plagiarized material. AI assistants cannot be relied upon because they are both inaccurate and misleading. (Birhane & McGann 2024; Bjarnason 2023; Chiang 2024; Hicks et al. 2024; Xu et al. 2024; Zhou et al. 2024)

  • LLMs are not reliable at summarizing documents and data. They emphasize the wrong points, include incorrect information, disregard context, and cause more work than if you had simply done the summary yourself. The idea that LLMs boost productivity is a myth. (Humlum & Vestergaard 2025; Mensik 2024; Wilson 2024)

  • GenAI / LLMs are prejudiced in just about every way they have been tested. They reproduce and intensify biases from their training data — including racism, sexism, and homophobia. They advise women to request significantly lower salaries than men and misclassify African American English as hateful. (Caliskan et al. 2017; Gilbert 2024; Hofmann et al. 2024; O’Hagan 2024; Sorokovikova et al. 2025)

  • GenAI / LLMs are polluting the internet and scientific research. AI-generated papers with incorrect arguments are indexed alongside reputable sources, threatening research integrity. (Haider et al. 2024; Koebler 2024; Roswell 2025)

  • AI companies have used information without authors’ or creators’ consent or compensation. AI exploits student labor by using submitted work to train products that are then sold back to universities without compensating students. I recommend you do not upload your own material to an LLM. (Low 2023; Marcus & Southern 2024; Weinberg 2024)

Remember: in almost all cases, your writing will only ever be read by exactly two people — you and me. Is it worth risking a failing grade when you could simply produce your own work?

Academic Integrity

Most students hold themselves to high ethical standards. When others engage in academic misconduct, it can reduce morale and diminish the meaning of being a Titan. The following policy is intended to support everyone in the Titan community.

California State University has a strict policy against academic fraud and dishonesty. It is the student’s responsibility to adhere to the guidelines regarding academic dishonesty as set forth in the university handbook. Policies can be found at: http://www.fullerton.edu/deanofstudents/conduct/policies-procedures.php.

These include, but are not limited to: California Code of Regulations Title 5, Section 41301; Student Rights and Responsibilities (UPS 300.000); Academic Dishonesty Policy (UPS 300.021).

The success of public health intervention relies on the public’s trust in the integrity of public health workers. Every act of dishonesty undermines the potential for public health officials and their research to be trusted. Probity should be considered a requisite characteristic of all persons working in the health sciences and is expected of Titans.

Students who inadvertently or negligently engage in academic dishonesty will be ineligible to receive credit for the related work. The student may be reassessed on all course material using a variety of modalities, including oral examination. Evidence of academic dishonesty will be reported to the Dean of Students and the CSUF Student Conduct team. The student will receive an F in the course upon determining that any incident was the result of willful intent or gross negligence.

Submitting deliverables with content that conflicts with or is disconnected from the course materials will be treated as evidence that the submission was generated without knowledge of the course or by the use of AI/LLMs. Respondus and Turnitin or similar software may be used to authenticate student work during online assessments.
